OPIOIDS

THE PROBLEM

Every day, more than 115 people in the United States die after overdosing on opioids.

What is an Opioid? An opioid is an opium-like compound that binds to one or more of the three opioid receptors of the body. Opioids have become an increasingly large problem in the United States, as both legal and illegal opioids are increasingly abused. With almost 32 times as worse of an opioid abuse problem as Japan, the United States ranks number 3 in opioid abuse. With such a large problem, many big pharmaceutical companies are still getting away with opioids being misprescribed and distributed. There needs to be a change in the way we treat and look at opioids, before the situation gets out of hand.
